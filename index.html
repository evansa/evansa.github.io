Projects

Over the years, I designed and developed a number of software applications. Some of them never saw the light of day, while others went on to make it big time - like a small Make Money Online spider app which was developed in a weekend and became a hit. Inspirations for a software usually comes from solving a problem or improving on what someone has already done. Coming up with a very unique idea for a software is very hard, just like every other things around these days... everyone is changing or modifying something that has already been done.

But recently, a few of my software have been making big hits. One of them is the humble Twitter App I developed late last year. When I designed it I aimed for it to do a very basic and still was useful and powerful. It covered the features that Twitter did not have or provided at that time. So this gave me not only the opportunity to try out the API, but also allowed me to mess about with Python, XML, JSON and a whole lot of MySQL designs. It was a fun experience that I really enjoyed.

As with most of my other software projects, I designed it on the train back home from work. I got home that Friday night and went straight to my computer. By mid-night, I had a working prototype. It was grabbing an RSS from Amazon, parsing it - using a great deal of Regular Expression and tweeting the result. This was fun because I could now sit back and watch the beast take on a life of it's own.

Over the weekend, I added all the features I wanted and polished the whole thing. As I had a dedicated Ubuntu Server, I was able to migrate the code over and had it running auto-pilot. It was fun. This continued for almost a year until late 2010 when Twitter decided to stop supporting basic API. A few months later, I finally got around to updating the code to use the new OAuth API. I also added the ability to tweet my RSS feed, SMS and Email notifications; which meant that I didn't have to rely on Twitterfeed any more. I could control my destiny as it were :)

Fast forward a year later - the twitter application has become so stable that many prominent companies and organisations now use it. The licensing terms are very liberal - so anyone can grab the code, modify as they see fit and even brand it. One thing I ask though is that you leave the copyright notice - I don't even want you linking back to my site - but I won't mind if you did.

The only thing that will cost you is the subscription to use the SMS notification feature. Not only did I invest a great deal of time into the application API, I also invested some amount of cash. This made it possible to use the real-time notifications. Because of the way I designed the application, you can pick and choose which of the methods to call once you've initialised the object. But if however, you need help with any of the features, I can always help you to get it running.
